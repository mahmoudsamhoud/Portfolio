PLATO's PIZZA SALES ANALYSIS

Check the project on GitHub

Introduction

Plato’s Pizza is a Greek-inspired pizza place in New Jersey. They have 15 tables and 60 seats. An assumption is made, based on the data, that this is related to dine-in orders only. Mario Maven, the restaurant’s manager, has been collecting transaction data for the past year. He is requesting for assistance to increase sales and increase operational efficiency.

Objectives

We need to analyze key indicators for our pizza sales data to gain insights into our business performance. Specifically, the following metrics:

  • Total Revenue
  • Average Order Value
  • Total Pizzas Sold
  • Total Orders
  • Average Pizzas Per Order

Also we would like to visualize various aspects of our pizza sales data to gain insights and understand key trends.

  • Daily Trend for Total Orders
  • Weekly Trend for Total Orders
  • Monthly Trend for Total Orders
  • Percentage of Sales by Pizza Category
  • Percentage of Sales by Pizza Size
  • Total Pizzas Sold by Pizza Category
  • Top 5 Sellers by Revenue, Total Quantity and Total Orders
  • Lowest 5 Sellers by Revenue, Total Quantity and Total Orders

About Dataset

This pizza sales dataset contains 48620 transaction records of 12 relevant features:

  1. order_id: Unique identifier for each order placed by a table

  2. order_details_id: Unique identifier for each pizza placed within each order (pizzas of the same type and size are kept in the same row, and the quantity increases)

  3. pizza_id: Unique key identifier that ties the pizza ordered to its details, like size and price

  4. quantity: Quantity ordered for each pizza of the same type and size

  5. order_date: Date the order was placed (entered into the system prior to cooking & serving)

  6. order_time: Time the order was placed (entered into the system prior to cooking & serving)

  7. unit_price: Price of the pizza in USD

  8. total_price: unit_price * quantity

  9. pizza_size: Size of the pizza (Small, Medium, Large, X Large, or XX Large)

  10. pizza_type: Unique key identifier that ties the pizza ordered to its details, like size and price

  11. pizza_ingredients: ingredients used in the pizza as shown in the menu (they all include Mozzarella Cheese, even if not specified; and they all include Tomato Sauce, unless another sauce is specified)

  12. pizza_name: Name of the pizza as shown in the menu

Analysis Method

The dataset was imported from csv file into a database using MySQL and then analyzed using SQL queries. Each query answers one of the objectives mentioned above. Same analyses were performed and visualized by an interactive dashboard using Power BI.

Results and Findings

  • Total Revenue: $ 817.86 K
  • Average Order Value: $38.31
  • Total Pizzas Sold: 49,574
  • Total Orders: 21,350
  • Average Pizzas Per Order: 2.32 pizzas
  • Daily Trend for Total Orders

    The busiest time of the day is between 12pm and 1pm which is lunch time and between 17pm and 18pm which is dinner time. KPIs for lunch time (12 pm : 1 pm) are 4,975 total orders, $217.94K revenue, 13,189 pizzas were sold of avg 2.65 pizzas per order, and avg order value $43.81. KPIs for dinner time (5pm : 6 pm) are 4,735 total orders, $175.53K revenue, 10,628 pizzas were sold of avg 2.24 pizzas per order, and avg order value $37.07.

  • Weekly Trend for Total Orders

    The busiest day of the week is Friday with 3,538 total orders, $136K revenue, 8,242 pizzas were sold of avg 2.33 pizzas per order, and avg order value $38.46.

  • Monthly Trend for Total Orders

    The busiest month of the year is July with 1,935 total orders and $72.56K revenue, although the range between minimum total orders/revenue (October) and maximum total orders/revenue (July) is 289 orders / $8.5K revenue.

  • Percentage of Sales by Pizza Category

    the contribution of each pizza category to sales is almost the same

  • Percentage of Sales by Pizza Size

    The most sold pizza size is Large with $375.32K (45.89% of total revenue), 18,956 pizzas within 12,736 orders of 1.49 avg pizzas per order and avg $29.47 order value. The lowest sold pizza size is XXL with $1.1k revenue, 28 pizzas within 28 orders (1 pizza per order), and avg order value $35.95.

  • Total Pizzas Sold by Pizza Category

    The category with most pizzas sold is Classic, 14,888 pizza (30% of total pizzas sold). Supreme category with 11,987 pizzas sold (24.18%). Veggie category with 11,649 pizzas sold (23.5%). Chicken category with 11,050 pizzas sold (22.29%)

Conclusion

Pizza orders peak during lunch time 12 pm to 1 pm and during dinner time 5 pm to 6 pm. Pizza orders and revenue maintain an upward trajectory from the beginning of the week on Sunday and peak on Friday before it starts dropping, this means most people prefer to eat pizza towards and during the weekend. Large size is the most sold size which means it has to be available all the time.
