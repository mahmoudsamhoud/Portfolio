Cold Extrusion Simulator

Introduction

Cold extrusion is a compressive forming process, also known as push-through, which is carried out at room temperature using a billet/slug as the starting material. During this process, the deforming material undergoes deformation heating, which involves the conversion of deformation work to heat at several hundred degrees. The process involves applying pressure to the enclosed billet in a stationary die using a punch, which can be applied partially or completely as per the requirements. Based on the punch and die design and the resulting material flow, cold extrusion can be classified into three major processes: forward extrusion, backward extrusion, and lateral extrusion.

Objectives

The objective of the project is to create a program that accurately simulates calculations for cold extrusion. This program will help determine the following factors:

  1. tonnage required for the press

  2. forces acting on the workpiece

  3. initial dimensions of the workpiece (diameter and height)

  4. die assembly

given the following data:

  1. product type.

  2. workpiece material

  3. dimensions of the final product
